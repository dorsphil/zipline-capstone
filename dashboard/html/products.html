Delivered Products & Medical Importance

This page analyzes the types of medical products delivered by Zipline and their role in supporting routine healthcare services and emergency medical response across Ghana.

Inventory Composition: Product Types Delivered

This chart breaks down deliveries by broad medical product categories, such as vaccines, essential medicines, blood products, and emergency supplies,revealing that Vaccines (51.6%) and Medical supplies (46.9%) constitute the overwhelming majority of Zipline's operations.

Interpretation:
The prominence of vaccines and essential medical products underscores Zipline's role in supporting preventive and routine healthcare, not just emergency response. These deliveries are foundational to public health outcomes, particularly for immunization programs and chronic care continuity.

Insight:
By ensuring consistent access to routine medical supplies, Zipline helps reduce stockouts, missed vaccinations, and treatment delays; issues that usually affect rural and remote communities.

Operational Tiering: Average Delivery Time by Product Type

This vertical bar chart compares the average delivery times for various medical categories, demonstrating clear operational tiering based on clinical urgency. Blood products lead with the fastest average delivery time of 43.0 minutes, followed by Medical Supplies at 57.4 minutes and Vaccines at 91.1 minutes.

Interpretation:
Faster delivery times for emergency-related products indicate operational prioritization, where time-critical items are dispatched with urgency. More stable delivery times for routine products reflect optimized scheduling without compromising reliability.

Insight:
This differentiation shows operational maturity. Zipline's system does not treat all deliveries equally; it dynamically balances speed and efficiency based on clinical urgency, aligning logistics with clinical decisions with patient needs.
