Delivered Products & Medical Importance

This page analyzes the types of medical products delivered by Zipline and their role in supporting routine healthcare services and emergency medical response across Ghana.

Product Types Delivered

This chart breaks down deliveries by broad medical product categories, revealing that Vaccines (51.6%) and Medical supplies (46.9%) constitute the majority of Zipline's operations.

The prominence of vaccines and essential medical products underscores Zipline's role in supporting preventive and routine healthcare. Ensuring people, especially children, in rural sectors have access to quality vaccines and essential medical supplies.

Insight:
By ensuring consistent access to routine medical supplies, Zipline helps reduce stockouts, missed vaccinations, and treatment delays; issues that usually affect rural and remote communities.

Critical Care Demand: Emergency Delivery Breakdown

This chart ranks the specific products requested during emergency flights by volume.

Products such as fluids and blood products dominate emergency deliveries reflecting their critical role in treating severe injuries, childbirth complications, and sudden medical crises.
The long tail of smaller-volume emergency products highlights the breadth of emergencies supported, even when individual product demand is low.

Insight:
This demonstrates that Zipline's emergency logistics system supports a wide range of life-saving interventions, enabling facilities to respond to diverse and unpredictable medical emergencies with confidence.

Operational Tiering: Average Delivery Time by Product Type

This bar chart compares the average delivery times for various medical categories, showing clear operational tiering based on clinical urgency. Blood products lead with the fastest average delivery time of 43.0 minutes, followed by Medical Supplies at 57.4 minutes and Vaccines at 91.1 minutes.

Faster delivery times for emergency-related products indicate operational prioritization, where time-critical items are dispatched with urgency.

Insight:
The differentiation dynamically balances speed and efficiency based on clinical urgency, aligning logistics with clinical decisions and with patient needs.
